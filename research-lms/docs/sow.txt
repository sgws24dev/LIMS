1.	Executive Summary
CUSTOMER seeks to procure and implement an enterprise‑grade Laboratory Management System (LMS), to support the digital transformation of its research laboratories and core facilities. The system shall unify operational workflows, asset lifecycle management, scheduling, financial
processes, and data‑driven decision‑making across CUSTOMER’s expanding scientific ecosystem.
The LMS must be AI‑powered, IoT‑enabled, MCP‑compliant, and designed for multi‑institution scalability. It shall integrate seamlessly with CUSTOMER’s identity systems, ERP/finance, HRIS,
calendar systems, ticketing platforms, and document repositories. The vendor shall deliver a secure, highly available, user‑friendly, audit‑ready platform suitable for long‑term institutional needs.


2.	Background
CUSTOMER operates complex, high‑performance laboratory environments supporting robotics, AI research, computer science, computer vision, and interdisciplinary scientific work. These
environments include advanced scientific instruments, specialized workspaces, core facilities, and diverse workflows across multiple stakeholders (students, researchers, PIs, faculty, technicians,
administrators).
Due to rapid growth in research output and infrastructure, CUSTOMER requires a centralized platform capable of:
•	Consolidating scheduling, workflows, and sample/service management
•	Managing equipment lifecycle, usage, maintenance, and compliance
•	Enforcing training prerequisites and access rules
•	Automating invoicing, billing, and cost recovery
•	Providing real‑time and historical insights via dashboards and analytics
•	Supporting multi‑facility, multi‑institution collaboration
•	Enabling AI‑driven insights, automation, and predictive maintenance
 

The initiative establishes a modern, scalable LMS foundation to serve current and future research demands.
3.	Project Objectives


The LMS shall:

1.	Digitize and standardize laboratory operations across core facilities.
2.	Optimize utilization of instruments, rooms, and resources.
3.	Enhance transparency through scheduling, audit trails, and reporting.
4.	Improve compliance with institutional, fiscal, and safety controls.
5.	Enable AI‑driven insights for maintenance, workflows, and resource allocation.
6.	Integrate with CUSTOMER systems (identity, ERP, HRIS, calendars, ticketing).
7.	Support growth, multi‑institution expansion, and future research centers.
8.	Provide a unified user experience via web and mobile applications.



4.	System Architecture Requirements
The LMS architecture shall be:
4.1	API‑First & Modular
•	Microservices preferred
•	All major functions exposed via REST/GraphQL APIs
•	Webhooks/events for downstream systems

4.2	MCP (Model Context Protocol) Support
•	Native support for MCP server and client modes
•	Secure tool calling
•	Structured context retrieval
•	Full auditability of AI‑driven actions
 
4.3	Cloud‑Hosted or Hybrid
•	Vendor may propose SaaS, hosted, or hybrid deployment
•	All deployment models must meet CUSTOMER security and residency policies

4.4	Scalability

The LMS must scale across:

•	Thousands of users
•	Thousands of pieces of equipment & instruments
•	Multiple labs and institutions
•	High‑volume transactions and analytics


4.5	Disaster Recovery & Resilience
•	The architecture must include documented disaster recovery capabilities
•	Support for geographic redundancy where available
•	Defined RPO/RTO targets aligned with CUSTOMER continuity requirements
•	Automated backup, failover, and recovery procedures
•	Regular DR testing and audit reporting

4.6	MCP Security Architecture
•	Mandatory authentication and authorization for all MCP tool calls and interactions
•	Role‑based access control (RBAC) defining which users, agents, or services can invoke specific MCP tools
•	Clear definition of allowed vs. prohibited AI actions, especially for operations affecting equipment, safety, or configuration
•	Encrypted transportation channels for MCP communication
•	Comprehensive audit trails capturing tool invoked, parameters, outputs, timestamps, actor identity, and approval steps
•	Mandatory human approval gates for high‑risk or system‑impacting MCP actions
•	Integration with CUSTOMER security monitoring, including anomaly detection, log retention, and incident response processes
